1. What are cookies?

An “internet cookie” (also known as a “browser cookie” or “HTTP cookie”) is a small text file stored on the computer, mobile phone or other device from which the Internet is accessed. A cookie consists of two parts: name and value. Technically, only the web server that sent the cookie can access it again when the user returns to the associated site.

Cookies do not in themselves require personal data and, in most cases, the information collected does not identify users. However, they may capture information such as IP address, geolocation, operating system, device resolution, visited pages, duration of visits and the way the Website is used. Based on these details we can:

  • adapt the Website’s presentation (language, resolution and layout);
  • display personalised advertising according to your interests;
  • create anonymous statistics that help us improve structure and content;
  • maintain web security.

Depending on their lifespan, cookies may be:

  • Session cookies – deleted automatically when the browser is closed;
  • Persistent cookies – stored on the device until they expire or are manually removed.

During a visit, the necessary Cookies are installed automatically. Analytics and marketing Cookies are activated only if your browser permits it. The Meta Pixel implementation checks the Global Privacy Control signal and the Do Not Track setting; if they are enabled, marketing events are not sent.

Analytics and marketing Cookies allow us to:

  • measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns and prevent repetitive ads;
  • understand user journeys within booking forms to fix bottlenecks.

Third parties may set their own anonymous cookies to measure specific campaigns or personalise the experience. The Website cannot access those cookies, just as third parties cannot access the Cookies we set.
